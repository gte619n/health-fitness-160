Tesseta

The mark — concept

A tessera is a single tile in a mosaic. The mark is built from nine square tiles on a 3×3 grid: eight olive tiles forming a ring, one tile absent at the center. The gap is the point. A health record is hundreds of small tiles, and the picture only resolves when you step back. The missing center tile also reads as a subtle negative-space focal point, and keeps the mark from being a generic grid icon.
